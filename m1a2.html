M1A2 (Abrams)

Wiki
Full
  • Playstyle
  • About

When playing as the Abrams, facing your armor towards the enemy is essential in surviving. The front makes up the bulk of the MBTs armored belt. Conflicts from the rear or side are risky at best, or straight deadily. It has range a finder allowing fast ranging, this means having several hundred meters of stand off can be managed with relative ease. The Abrams makes use of a stablized weapon platform for both commander and gunner, staying mobile is important in urban areas when against infantry. When facing other tanks, do not rely on the stablization while moving, rather stop or slow down and allow the gunner to zero their shots in. Like any tracked vehicle, the Abrams can be disabled with just one shot if tracked, forcing a dismount/risky repair. Pushing with friendly infantry elements in front of or round you is important in preventing being chased down by enemy LAT/HAT crews and risking becoming tracked.


Created in 1970s for the United States Army, it is classified as main battle tank. Seeing use in several conflicts and countries over the last few decades.
