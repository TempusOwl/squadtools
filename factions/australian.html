About Faction
Weapons
The Australian make use of spectre optics for most of their kits, giving them an edge in infantry battles. Their rifles have high rate of fire and moderate recoil.
Vehicles
PMV can shuttle an entire squad making it a power choice to capture back cap or rotate. The ASLAV carries more ammo than a BTR, edging out against BTRs in battle, though its optic requires learning. M1A1 is the M1A2 (but without a loader MG). MRH carries 1500c giving it an edge over UH-60m.
Strengths
Their optics and slightly better IFVs make them a hard force to fight 1v1.
Weaknesses
They are predictable to play against, having 1 type of IFV and tank. They lack an armor hunter vehicle (IE: Simir Kornet/BPMs AGMs etc..), so they heavily rely on their armor for map control on vehicle heavy maps.
Squad Role Tier List
Command & Support
Section Leader [S]
The Australian have S tier SL kits, giving the SL a foregrip-pod (aka bipod) + optic that can be used to steady the weapon and dish out accurate hits at range.
Medic
Medic is a must-have pick, allowing you to revive faster and sustain pushes. The bottom one has an optic (limit of 1), whereas the second has iron sights + grenade (limit of 1). You can have a total of two medics, even being forced to use iron sights do not matter as the value of reviving and healing can be massive with a second medic.
Lead Pilot/Crewman [D]
Carry only two mags, making them not great for combat. They do carry spectre optics though, making them S-tier. IMPORTANT: These roles require 3 people for rallies!
Squad Kits
Rifleman [A]
30 round mags with moderate recoil, with a high rate of fire (good for CQB). It has moderate recoil, though single shot should to used on targets beyond 15m. Riflemen carry ammo bags, making them the single biggest asset on the battlefield (besides HAT/SLs) to help resupply pushes, rallies and anti-armor.
Automatic Rifleman [C]
It is main weapon most kits have, it has 30 round mag and plays similar to US Weapons. The recoil can be controlled, unlike the G3. At any range greater than 30m meters, tap firing is ideal with this weapon. This weapon has a far greater rate of fire than OPFOR factions, giving you an edge in CQB.
Fire Support
Automatic Rifleman [S]
Powerful combo, using the F89 Minimi w/ Specter sight. Being a higher caliber bullet, it does more damage and suffers less damage drop off at longer ranges. It has high recoil, though its bipod can heavily reduce it. Many guns in this class lack an optic, making this a hidden gem.
Grenadier [A]
Carry solve explode on impact frags, and fast deploy smokes. A power kit to take out enemies behind cover or within buildings.
Light Anti-Tank [B]
Anti-Tank crews are equipped with LAWs which are only capable of tracking tanks, or disabling engines on light attack vehicles. The Australian faction has HK417 marksman rifles, it does not have modifiable zero, making it mid-tier.
Marksman [C]
Equiped with a better optic, this kit can see enemies at ranges beyond 400m. It has a limited mag sizes and no ammo bag. This optic can not be zero'ed.
Specialist
HAT [S]
Is single most powerful kit, allowing you to destroy vehicles.
Automatic Rifleman [S]
Australians are known best for their specter + machine gun combo that is deadly. The HATs for Australian are mid-tier with simple ranging optic and good damage. They are capable of putting out smoking shells (similar to mortar smokes).
Combat Engineer [C]
Can be used to place mines, repair and dig things up/down faster. The kit can also place C4 on objects like a radio (though you must dig it down 1 level before placing the explosive to cause it to bleedout). This kit often relies on infantry support to finish off bleeding out radios and armored vehicles.
